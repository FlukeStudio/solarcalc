About SolarCalc

Why We Built This

Every other "free solar calculator" online does the same thing: asks for your email or phone number, then sells you to installers. We got tired of it.

SolarCalc is different: no email, no phone, no sales calls. You enter your numbers, you get your estimate, you leave. That's it.

Our Data

Every number you see comes from authoritative public sources:

  • Peak Sun Hours — NREL PVWatts v8 / National Solar Radiation Database (2026)
  • Electricity Rates — US Energy Information Administration (July 2026)
  • Installation Costs — SEIA / EnergySage Q1 2026 Marketplace Data
  • Federal Tax Credit — IRS Section 25D, 30% ITC through 2032
  • State Incentives — DSIRE, state energy offices, utility filings
  • Battery Costs — EnergySage marketplace (Feb 2026), NREL ATB

Is the estimate accurate?

For a standard south-facing roof with no significant shading, our estimates are within ±10% of professional installer quotes. This is an independent estimate, not a binding quote. Solar installation costs vary with roof complexity, local permit fees, and installer competition — always get 3+ quotes before deciding.

How We Make Money

SolarCalc is free. We may earn a small commission if you choose to compare quotes through EnergySage (at no cost to you). We do not sell your data. We do not run ads that track you.
